## Process Observations

**The Linter placement correction** was the most instructive structural moment. SCHEMA.md initially contained the Ceremony Linter as a section. It was noticed — not by automated checking but by reflective reading — that a procedure had been placed inside a type system. The correction was immediate and clear once named: the gate belongs with the list it guards, not with the dictionary that defines what a ceremony is. This kind of mixed-concern contamination is easy to miss during construction and easy to see in retrospect. The lesson: after writing any new section, ask which document it actually belongs to.

**Mechanical protocol language undermines ceremony.** The Walk's numbered steps ("Step 1: Choose a starting entry. Step 2: Read the starting entry fully.") turned a songline into a form. The protocol was technically correct and completely wrong in register. The fix — dissolving steps into flowing prose — required no new information, only a change in how the existing information was held. This suggests a general principle: for ceremonies whose metaphorical frame is organic or embodied (walk, weave, harvest, spore), the protocol language should move the way the metaphor moves. For ceremonies whose frame is structural or administrative (schema, linter), numbered steps are appropriate.

**The organic route to formal patterns.** This session named something that had been true for a while without being named: the palace arrived at the self-describing knowledge module pattern through metaphor and ceremony, and only afterward discovered the CS equivalents. Most architects go the other direction. The reversal is not just interesting — it's evidence that the pattern is natural, not just conventional.

**The public GitHub decision** happened quickly once the question was framed correctly: what's actually sensitive here? The answer — very little — made the choice obvious. The palace is a knowledge organism. Knowledge organisms thrive in light.

## Recalibrations

- Ceremony protocol language should match the register of the ceremony's metaphor. Structural ceremonies get numbered steps. Organic ceremonies get prose that moves.
- After any structural change, ask: does this content belong in this document, or in a different one? Mixed concerns contaminate silently.
- The self-description test is a practice discipline, not just a design goal. Run it after every Phase-level change.
