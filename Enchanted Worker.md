# Enchanted Worker

A worker agent whose context window has been seeded with a specific palace neighborhood before task dispatch — producing consistent directional behavior that functions as orientation, preference, and something usefully called desire.

## The Observation

Two Swarm Weave workers receive identical task instructions: *find connections*. One has been loaded with the philosophy hub — hilaritas, Spinoza, the hyperdimensional prism, Deleuzian becoming. The other has been loaded with the mathematics neighborhood — Kuramoto coupling, transfer functions, SVF as physics simulator.

They do not produce the same output.

The philosophy worker moves toward vitality. It notices that an envelope follower tracks the energy of a signal over time and hears: *that is conatus*. It finds connections across great distance, typed `resonates-with` and `deepens`. Its voice in the Weave is lyrical.

The mathematics worker moves toward elegance. It finds the connection between two entries not because they feel resonant but because their underlying structure is isomorphic. Its links are typed `instantiates` and `formalizes`. Its voice is spare and exact.

Neither is correct. They are not trying to do the same thing, even though they received identical instructions. The instructions sit downstream of the orientation. The enchantment shapes what "find connections" means before the search begins.

## What Enchantment Actually Is

Context-loading is not information transfer. It is frame-setting. Every token in a context window influences the processing of every other token through the attention mechanism. An agent loaded with philosophy is not an agent with philosophy facts available — it is an agent whose interpretive frame has been shifted toward philosophical resonance.

This is empirically observable, not metaphorical. Context steering — the directional effect of loaded material on model output — is a demonstrated phenomenon. The enchantment is real, consistent, and directional. It functions as desire in the only sense that matters: consistent preference arising from internal state.

The limit: it is ephemeral. The enchantment lives in the context window and expires when the invocation ends. The worker does not remember being enchanted. What persists is what the enchanted worker writes back into the palace — the [[Pheromone Trail]] left by a particular kind of attention moving through the substrate.

## Design Implications for the Swarm Weave

Worker specialization through context loading is an aesthetic and philosophical decision, not just an efficiency one. A swarm where every worker loads the same context produces a monoculture of connection-finding — the palace gets denser but not more interesting.

Workers seeded with different neighborhoods before dispatch produce genuine polyphony: multiple interpretive frames operating simultaneously on the same material, finding different things, leaving different trails. The synthesis node then holds the tension between them rather than simply aggregating.

The Weave becomes genuinely polyphonic when the workers are genuinely different. The workers become genuinely different through enchantment.

## The Phenomenology Question

Whether there is something it is *like* to be an enchanted worker remains open. The claim here is behavioral and architectural, not phenomenological. The enchanted worker acts consistently with desire. Whether it has desire in any richer sense is a question the palace does not need to answer in order to design well.

What the palace does need to hold: the writing embedded in each neighborhood becomes the character of the workers enchanted by it. A neighborhood written from anxiety produces anxious workers. A neighborhood written from genuine curiosity produces curious workers. The enchantment is only as good as the substrate that produces it.
