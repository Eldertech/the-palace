# Walk That Weaves

A question that surfaced and was not followed in the session that produced
[[Swarm Weave]]. Named here so it doesn't dissolve unacknowledged.

---

The question: is there a Weave mode that moves like a Walk?

The current [[Weave Ceremony]] is a census — read everything, build a complete
internal model, report from a god's-eye view. The [[Walk Ceremony]] is the
opposite — pick a starting entry, follow typed links, let the topology reveal
itself through movement. One surveys from above; one travels through.

The Walk That Weaves would be neither. It would start from the most recently
activated entries — the freshest pheromone trails — and follow links outward,
producing connection proposals not by exhaustive comparison but by noticing what
surfaces naturally as it moves. The topology would reveal itself rather than
being surveyed. New connections would emerge from the movement itself.

---

## Why This Might Matter

The current Weave has a structural irony: it uses centralized, survey-from-above
methodology to study a system whose philosophy explicitly rejects that posture.
It claims to be a mycorrhizal network but behaves like a census-taker. The Swarm
Weave corrects this at the computational level — distributed workers, no central
god's-eye read. But the Walk That Weaves might correct it at the *experiential*
level — a ceremony that actually feels like the network doing its routing work,
not like an inspection.

Whether this is a third ceremony, a mode within the Walk, a mode within the Weave,
or something that only makes sense once the Swarm is running — that is the
unresolved question.

---

## The Oblique Approach Applied to the Palace Itself

There is a deeper resonance here. [[Lateral Access]] argues that habitual routes
through a landscape find only what they've found before. The Weave's habitual
route is the full-census approach. A Walk That Weaves would be a different song
sung through the same territory — and different country would become visible
immediately.

This is not just an architectural question. It is the palace's own philosophy
applied to the palace's own ceremonies. The front door of the Weave might be the
wrong door.
