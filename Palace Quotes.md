# Palace Quotes

A living collection of quotes that have landed in the palace — lines that carry enough weight to persist. Not a reference list. A resonance library. Each quote earns its place by arriving in the middle of real work and refusing to leave.

---

> "The student who aims at correctness will remain a student. The one who aims at understanding will become a teacher."

— Epictetus (paraphrased)

*Origin: arose in a conversation on whether Claude skills should be defined through desire, reward, and punishment framing — March 2026. The quote crystallized the contrast between compliance and understanding as learning targets.*

---

> "A rule without understanding produces compliance. Understanding without rules produces wisdom."

— Confucian tradition (paraphrased)

*Origin: same conversation, same moment. Emerged as the complementary face of the Epictetus quote — two sides of a single insight about what teaching is actually for. Seeded the [[Palace Philosophies]] entry on Confucianism.*
